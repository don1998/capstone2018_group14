How does it work?

What was our Objective?

We set out to achieve the objective of proving that an NPC trained with machine learning can provide a more challenging experience to a video-game player. As a proof of concept we made a small combat-based game with a single player versus an enemy to showcase our implementation.

What is AI and Machine Learning?

AI stands for Artificial Intelligence. This is the theory and development of computer systems that are able to perform tasks that usually require human intelligence, such as visual perception, speech recognition, decision-making, and translation between languages. Machine learning is an application of AI that focuses on using statistical techniques to provide computers with the ability to 'learn', i.e. progressively improve performance on a particular task.

What did we do?

We used the Unity platform to design our game environment, which comprises of the setting in which our player and enemy will be fighting as well as other game objects and assets. The models used for the player and enemy, along with other assets were obtained from the Unity asset store as well as other online open source libraries. Our game mimics a boss fight in a typical combat game with our player being a female warrior, going up against the Monkey King.

Once the environment was created we then began training the Monkey King against a hard-coded opponent. This training essentially involved our hard-coded opponent, using observations it took from the game environment to make decisions based on the conditions we defined as it fights against the Monkey King. The Monkey King performs actions of its own and based on these actions it is either rewarded or punished as we see fit.

How it Works

Brains are entities that define specific states and action spaces. They are responsible for the actions each of the connected "agents", such as the player and the monkey king, will take. In our implementation we created a Heuristic Player Brain, Player Brain, Internal Enemy Brain, External Enemy Brain, Heuristic Enemy Brain.

  • Heuristic Player Brain and Heuristic Enemy Brain: These brains use their observations of the game environment and hard-coded instructions to carry out actions.
  • Player Brain: This brain uses input from an actual player to carry out its actions. eg: input from a keyboard.
  • Internal Enemy Brain: This is the brain of an enemy that has been trained to fight against our hard-coded opponent.
  • External Enemy Brain: This is the brain of an enemy whose actions are made externally by Tensorflow.
